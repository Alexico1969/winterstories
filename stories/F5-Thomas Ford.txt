The Midnight Sleigh Ride
The Midnight Sleigh Ride
by You
Alex never really understood why everyone in his family was so obsessed with Christmas. His parents and his little sister Molly kept decorating and singing like it was their job, but he just didn’t feel anything special about it. He usually pretended to be excited so he wouldn’t ruin it for anyone, but inside he felt kind of disconnected from the whole thing. Maybe it was because he was adopted and still felt like he didn’t totally fit in yet, even though his parents reminded him a thousand times that he belonged. On Christmas Eve, the house smelled like cinnamon and pine, and everyone else seemed extra cheerful while Alex mostly just felt tired. After everyone went to bed, he stayed awake, staring at the ceiling and wondering why Christmas just didn’t hit him the way it hit everyone else.
He wasn’t expecting anything weird to happen, but then he heard this soft jingling sound from outside, like actual bells. At first he rolled his eyes because it had to be the neighbors or something, but the sound kept getting louder until he couldn’t ignore it. Curiosity got the best of him, so he went to the window and pushed the curtain aside. That’s when he saw something he absolutely knew couldn’t be real: a huge red sleigh floating down into his backyard like it was no big deal. Reindeer stomped on the snow and breathed out clouds of white mist, and everything sparkled like it was straight out of a Christmas movie. Alex’s heart was pounding so hard he felt like it might shake him awake if this was a dream.
He slipped on his jacket and boots and crept outside, trying not to wake anyone. The cold air hit his face instantly, but the excitement sort of canceled it out. Standing in the backyard, he finally got a close look at the sleigh, and honestly, he didn’t know whether to laugh or run inside. A man in a red coat climbed down, brushing snow off his gloves like he’d just finished a shift at work instead of… whatever this was. When the man turned around, Alex froze, because it was seriously Santa Claus. Not mall Santa or cheesy movie Santa — but someone who actually looked like he belonged in the sky with flying reindeer.
Santa smiled like he had been expecting him and said, “Took you long enough.” Alex didn’t know what to say, because obviously Santa couldn’t be real, and yet he was right there talking to him. Before he could do more than stare, the front door creaked open and Molly stepped out, rubbing her eyes. She saw the sleigh and Santa and immediately gasped so loudly Alex almost laughed. Santa told them both he needed a little help delivering presents because it was a busy night, and Alex could feel Molly vibrating with excitement next to him. Alex wasn’t sure, but something in Santa’s voice made him feel like this was meant for him more than anyone.
When the three of them climbed into the sleigh, Alex felt something shift inside him, almost like he’d been waiting for this without realizing it. The reindeer took off so fast he grabbed the side of the sleigh, but after a few seconds, the fear turned into a wild kind of joy. They flew over neighborhoods lit up with colored lights and houses that looked like little gingerbread pieces from above. Santa handed them each a present to drop into a chimney, and even though Alex had no idea what he was doing, Santa didn’t seem worried at all. Molly kept laughing and pointing out things below them, but Alex was quiet, taking it all in. For once, he felt like he belonged exactly where he was supposed to be.
Somewhere over the city, Santa leaned over and said something that Alex knew he’d remember for the rest of his life. He said families aren’t about matching or being perfect — they’re about choosing each other, again and again. Alex didn’t respond right away, but he felt that sentence settle into him like warmth spreading from inside. When they finally flew back home, the sky was turning a bit lighter, and Alex wished the night didn’t have to end. Santa said goodbye with a wink, promising they’d meet again someday. The sleigh lifted off, bells jingling, and Alex and Molly stood there until it disappeared into the stars.
The house felt different when they walked back inside, even though nothing had changed except him. For the first time, Alex understood why his family loved Christmas so much — it wasn’t the lights or decorations or presents. It was the feeling of belonging, of being part of something bigger than just yourself. When his parents came down in the morning and saw him actually smiling, they looked surprised but happy. Alex didn’t tell them everything, but he didn’t have to. He finally felt like he had a real place in their world, and that was the best gift he could’ve gotten.
